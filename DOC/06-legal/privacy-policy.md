## 4. Data Retention

### 4.1 Account Data

- **Active accounts:** Retained for as long as your account is active
- **Inactive accounts:** Deleted after 12 months of inactivity (with 30-day notice)
- **Deleted accounts:** Permanently deleted within 30 days

### 4.2 Contest Data

- **Active/Draft draws:** Retained until completion or deletion
- **Completed draws:** Retained for 1 year (for audit and legal purposes)
- **Participant data:** Deleted 30 days after draw completion (unless draw is kept for records)

### 4.3 Financial Data

- **Transaction records:** Retained for 7 years (tax and legal requirements)
- **Invoices:** Retained for 10 years (accounting requirements)

### 4.4 Support Communications

- **Support tickets:** Retained for 3 years
- **Chat logs:** Retained for 1 year

### 4.5 Backups

Data in backups is retained for 30 days and then permanently deleted.

## 5. Your Privacy Rights

### 5.1 General Rights (All Users)

**Access:** You can access your personal information through your account settings.

**Correction:** You can update your information at any time through account settings.

**Deletion:** You can request account deletion via account settings or by emailing privacy@cleack.io.

**Opt-out of Marketing:** Unsubscribe from marketing emails via the link in each email.

### 5.2 GDPR Rights (EU Users)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have additional rights under the GDPR:

**Right to Access:** Request a copy of your personal data.

**Right to Rectification:** Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.

**Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"):** Request deletion of your data.

**Right to Restriction:** Limit how we process your data.

**Right to Data Portability:** Receive your data in a machine-readable format.

**Right to Object:** Object to processing based on legitimate interests.

**Right to Withdraw Consent:** Withdraw consent for data processing at any time.

**Right to Lodge a Complaint:** File a complaint with your local data protection authority.

**To exercise these rights, email privacy@cleack.io with your request.**

### 5.3 CCPA Rights (California Users)

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

**Right to Know:** Request information about data we collect, use, and share.

**Right to Delete:** Request deletion of your personal information.

**Right to Opt-Out of Sale:** We do not sell personal information, so this right is not applicable.

**Right to Non-Discrimination:** We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

**To exercise these rights, email privacy@cleack.io or call +33 1 23 45 67 89.**

### 5.4 Response Time

We will respond to your privacy requests within:
- **GDPR requests:** 30 days
- **CCPA requests:** 45 days
- **General requests:** 30 days

## 6. Security

### 6.1 How We Protect Your Data

We implement industry-standard security measures:

**Encryption:**
- Data in transit: TLS 1.3 (HTTPS)
- Data at rest: AES-256 encryption
- Database encryption (RDS encryption)
- S3 server-side encryption

**Authentication:**
- Password hashing (bcrypt with salt)
- JWT-based authentication
- OAuth2 for social media connections
- Two-factor authentication (2FA) available

**Access Control:**
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Principle of least privilege
- Employee access logging

**Monitoring:**
- 24/7 security monitoring
- Intrusion detection systems
- Automated vulnerability scanning
- Regular penetration testing

**Infrastructure:**
- Secure AWS infrastructure
- Firewalls and network segmentation
- DDoS protection (Cloudflare)
- Regular security updates

### 6.2 Your Responsibility

You are responsible for:
- Keeping your password secure
- Using strong passwords
- Enabling two-factor authentication
- Not sharing account credentials
- Logging out on shared devices

### 6.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will:
- Notify you within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
- Notify relevant authorities as required by law
- Provide details of the breach and steps taken
- Offer assistance to mitigate potential harm

## 7. Cookies and Tracking

### 7.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us provide and improve the Service.

### 7.2 Types of Cookies We Use

**Essential Cookies (Required):**
- Authentication and session management
- Security features
- Load balancing
- CSRF protection

**Functional Cookies (Optional):**
- Remember your preferences (language, theme)
- Pre-fill forms
- Improve user experience

**Analytics Cookies (Optional):**
- Google Analytics (anonymized IP)
- Mixpanel (product analytics)
- Error tracking (Sentry)

**Marketing Cookies (Optional):**
- Conversion tracking
- Retargeting ads (if you consent)

### 7.3 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie banner. You can:
- Accept all cookies
- Reject optional cookies (essential cookies remain)
- Customize cookie preferences

### 7.4 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through:
- Our cookie settings (available in footer)
- Browser settings (block/delete cookies)
- Third-party opt-out tools (e.g., Google Analytics Opt-out)

**Note:** Disabling essential cookies may impact Service functionality.

### 7.5 Do Not Track

We honor Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. When DNT is enabled, we disable optional analytics and marketing cookies.
